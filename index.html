Secure your data, even under pressure
A concept for an open-source all-in-one voice recording, camera, and file management app designed to keep your data safe in high-risk situations
Securely hide files, outsmart attackers, share encrypted data, and prevent loss under duress
Context
Problem

Data security has long been compromised by a fundamental issue: when individuals are forced into revealing their passwords or disabling the safety mechanisms under their control, the entire system becomes vulnerable. This inherent weakness, stemming from the reliance on individuals to safeguard their security credentials, remains unresolved despite advancements in both software and hardware technologies. The reality is stark—under pressure, whether through threats, manipulation, or brute force, the person who holds the keys to the system typically fails to maintain its security. This makes the individual the ultimate point of failure, no matter how advanced the system.

Solution

The solution we propose might seem straightforward: remove the user from the equation. In other words, allow the user to interact with the system but remove their control over access to it. This approach involves enabling the user to deliberately transfer their security credentials to a trusted third party. In scenarios where dangerous actors might try to force the user to grant access, this method could keep the data safe, as the user would be unable to comply even if they wanted to. The challenge lies in designing a system with the right combination of features and safety mechanisms that allow it to be effectively used while ensuring that access to the data can be regained once the danger has passed. A product that successfully achieves this would fundamentally change how data is recorded, captured, and stored in high-risk environments.

Product

While the proposed solution can be applied to securing data across various systems and industries, we have distilled this concept into a mobile app that features versatile data-capturing capabilities, including a sound recorder, camera, and file manager. The SaferVault concept wraps these functionalities within three safety features that seamlessly combine to protect user data across various extreme scenarios. With SaferVault, users can manage their own secure recording environments, back up and share encrypted data, and address situations where they may be forced to give away their passwords or biometric credentials. To top it off, the app’s streamlined, mobile-only design facilitates rapid development and deployment while providing ease of use with minimal components to safeguard and maintain.

Who is it for?

Investigative journalists often gather sensitive information that could endanger them if exposed.

Whistleblowers risk retaliation when exposing wrongdoing and may need to record evidence in a way that provides maximum protection if they are caught.

Activists and civic protesters documenting illegal activities, often by security personnel, are at risk of having their recordings seized or destroyed and may be forced to produce or delete their recordings.

Employees documenting exploitation need to protect their data from employers who may try to suppress it.

Law enforcement officials often collect sensitive information in the field and may face attempts to compromise their data.

Human rights defenders frequently document abuses in high-risk areas, where oppressive forces might try to access or destroy evidence.

Corporate and private investigators handling sensitive cases need to protect their findings from internal leaks or espionage.

Everyone else who wants to keep their data secure and hidden from unauthorized access in everyday scenarios.

Features
I Instance-based encryption

SaferVault enables users to create multiple recording instances, each safeguarded by a unique password. This feature ensures that the data collected within a specific instance is encrypted and accessible only through the corresponding password, ensuring that all recordings tied to other passwords remain hidden.

This compartmentalization offers flexibility across various scenarios, whether the user needs to protect sensitive business information, personal recordings, or crucial evidence in extreme situations. For instance, if a user is under duress, they can provide access to a less critical recording while keeping more sensitive data safely concealed.

II Encrypted export & import

From within SaferVault instances, users can export recordings and files as ordinary TXT files that contain encrypted data. On their own, these files are worthless, but they can be imported back into the same SaferVault installation and decrypted using the original password, within the same instance where they were created. Additionally, they can be sent to a different device, imported into SaferVault, and decrypted using the same password.

This feature provides great flexibility in risk-averse situations. Users can back up their encrypted data to the cloud or share it securely with others. Once the recipient retrieves the encrypted file and is made aware of the password (send via secure messaging apps, like Signal), they can import it into their SaferVault app and access its content.

III Guard mode

The Guard mode is a special instance which is designed for high-risk situations where users may be forced or pressured to reveal access to their recordings or files. This mode allows users to capture information without the ability to access it themselves, preventing the attacker from obtaining access to the data through the user.

Unlike a regular instance, the Guard mode instance requires pre-authorization by another individual. Once set up, the user can record or capture data without knowing the password, ensuring the recorded information is encrypted and stored securely on the device. Although the data can be exported as a TXT file, it remains inaccessible to the user (and attacker) without the third party’s involvement.

How to use?
1. Let someone else enter the password to activate the Guard mode

In high-risk scenarios where users may expect to be pressured to reveal sensitive information—such as when traveling through hostile environments, interviewing sensitive witnesses, or dealing with authorities who may demand access to devices—not having access to their own data can be a strategic advantage. The first step is for a trusted individual to authorize a Guard mode instance in the user’s SaferVault installation by entering a password that the user deliberately does not know. This is done physically, either by the user handing the device to the trusted individual or by the user taking possession of the already authorized device.

2. Use the Guard mode to record sensitive Information

With a Guard mode instance that is primed and ready, all recorded data is encrypted using a password unknown to the user. Even if an attacker attempts to force access to the information, their efforts will be futile, as the user cannot access the data themselves. This circumstance also creates a practical deterrent, discouraging the attacker from applying further force, recognizing it as a pointless effort.

3. Access recorded information through the person who knows the password

Once the danger has passed, the user can contact the person who knows the password to regain access to their recordings and files. Although the user cannot view the content within the Guard mode instance, they can list, rename, and export items as encrypted TXT files, just as in a regular SaferVault instance. These files can then be sent to the person who authorized the instance for decryption, or the password can be securely sent back to the user (via Signal, for example). With the password, the user can convert the Guard mode instance back into a regular one, allowing them to access and consume the information on their device.

Your data is securely sealed away, with the key held by someone you trust—accessible only when the threat is gone
Get involved

SaferVault is a practical solution designed to transform data security in high-risk environments. Since building a proof-of-concept application in 2020, we've laid the groundwork for a solution that could have a significant impact in the field. But there's still much work to be done.

We invite developers and digital security professionals to join us in realizing SaferVault's full potential. Your expertise can help us enhance and expand the app's capabilities, ensuring it meets the highest standards of security and usability. We also welcome members of the media community, human rights activists, legal professionals, and risk management experts to contribute to testing and refining the concept, aiming for maximum impact for the public good. We are committed to maintaining the open-source nature of the solution and continuously iterating based on community feedback.
